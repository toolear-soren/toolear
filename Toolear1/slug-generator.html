The Anatomy of a Perfect URL

Creating a slug isn't just about formatting; it's about strategy. Here are the rules for creating URLs that both users and search engines love:

1. Keep it Lowercase

While domain names are case-insensitive, the path (the slug) is not. On Linux servers, Page.html and page.html are different files. To avoid confusion and duplicate content issues, always use lowercase. Our tool handles this automatically.

2. Hyphens vs. Underscores

This is the golden rule of technical SEO. Google treats a hyphen as a word separator (like a space). It treats an underscore as a word joiner.
Correct: seo-tool-guide (Read as: "seo tool guide")
Incorrect: seo_tool_guide (Read as: "seotoolguide")
By using hyphens, you ensure Google can index individual keywords in your URL.

3. Remove "Stop Words"

Stop words are common words like "a", "the", "and", "or", "of". They add length to the URL without adding semantic value.
Title: "The 10 Best Tools for SEO"
Bad Slug: the-10-best-tools-for-seo
Good Slug: 10-best-seo-tools
Shorter URLs are easier to share, remember, and they look better in search snippets.

How It Works

Toolear converts your text using standard regular expressions (Regex). Here is the logic flow:

  1. Normalize: The input string is converted to lowercase characters.
  2. Sanitize: Any character that is NOT a letter, number, or space is removed. This strips out commas, quotes, emojis, and symbols.
  3. Replace: Any sequence of spaces (or existing underscores) is replaced with a single hyphen.
  4. Trim: Leading or trailing hyphens are removed to ensure the URL looks clean (e.g., no -hello-world-).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a URL Slug?
A slug is the unique identifying part of a web address, typically located at the very end of the URL. In the address toolear.space/slug-generator, the string slug-generator is the slug. It tells both users and search engines what the page is about.
Why use hyphens instead of underscores?
It's about how Google parses text. Google treats hyphens (-) as separators, meaning it sees "seo-tool" as two words: "seo" and "tool". It treats underscores (_) as joiners, so "seo_tool" is read as one unknown word "seotool". For maximum SEO benefit, always use hyphens.
Does this tool remove special characters?
Yes. Characters like @, #, $, %, &, and parentheses are removed because they either have special meanings in code (like # for anchors) or simply clutter the URL. We strictly allow only alphanumeric characters (a-z, 0-9) and hyphens.
How long should a slug be?
Short and punchy is best. Aim for 3 to 5 words. Include your primary keyword. Avoid "stop words" (a, the, on). Extremely long URLs can get truncated in search results and look suspicious to users.
