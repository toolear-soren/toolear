The "Decimal Separator War": Dot vs. Comma

Did you know that half the world writes numbers differently than the other half? This often causes confusion in international business and software development.

The Anglosphere (US, UK, Australia)

In English-speaking countries, a dot (.) is used as the decimal separator, and a comma (,) is used to group thousands.
Example: 12,345.67

Europe & South America

In Germany, Brazil, France, and many other countries, the roles are reversed. A comma (,) separates decimals, and a dot (.) or a space groups thousands.
Example: 12.345,67

The Indian Numbering System

India uses a unique system. Instead of grouping every 3 digits, they group the first 3, then every 2 digits afterwards (Lakhs and Crores).
Example: 12,34,567.89

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do some countries use dots instead of commas?
It is a historical convention. The UK and US adopted the dot (.) as the decimal separator, while many European countries adopted the comma (,). Toolear uses the browser's Intl.NumberFormat API to respect these regional differences automatically.
What is "Compact Notation"?
Compact notation is a way of writing large numbers using abbreviations. Instead of "1,000", it writes "1K". Instead of "1,000,000", it writes "1M". This is extremely useful for designing user interfaces where horizontal space is limited.
